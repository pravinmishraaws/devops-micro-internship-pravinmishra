# Task 1 — Understanding AWS & Free Tier
## Goal
Demonstrate understanding of AWS basics and Free Tier usage by answering the following questions in your own words (3–4 lines each).
### Answers
#### Question 1 — What is an AWS account, and why do you need it at this stage?
An AWS account is your entry point into Amazon's cloud platform — it gives you a unique identity to provision, manage, and pay for cloud resources like servers, storage, and databases. At this stage, I need one to move EpicReads' infrastructure off physical/on-prem systems and onto the cloud, the same way I've managed physical BSS/RAN infrastructure in telecom — except now provisioning happens through an API/console instead of a site visit.
---
#### Question 2 — What is AWS Free Tier, and how long does it last?
AWS Free Tier lets new users try core AWS services at no cost, within set usage limits, for 12 months from account creation. Some services (like limited DynamoDB or Lambda usage) are "always free," while others are free for 12 months or trial-based. It's meant to give learners and startups hands-on experience without upfront billing risk.
---
#### Question 3 — Name three AWS Free Tier services and their free usage limits.
1. **EC2 (t2.micro/t3.micro):** 750 hours/month free for 12 months — enough to run one instance continuously.
2. **S3:** 5GB of standard storage free for 12 months.
3. **RDS:** 750 hours/month of a db.t2.micro/t3.micro instance, free for 12 months.
